Subject: [PATCH v5 0/9] xen: Add NUMA-aware claim sets for domains

Introduction
------------

Dear Reviewers,

Thank you for your feedback on the previous series. This update builds
on that feedback to support installing multi-node claim sets atomically.

The patch structure has been revamped, and the implementation has been
improved based on your suggestions.

Until now, this series only implemented designating a single NUMA node
as the claim target of a domain.

The series starts with detailed design documentation as a basis for any
questions you may have. It is meant to introduce the concepts of the
design and should be best seen as a handbook for the review, rather
than as a final specification.

It is followed by three small patches from v4, improved and split based
on your suggestions, plus a new fix related to offlining pages in the
presence of claims.

The next commit splits d->outstanding_pages into two variables, one for
global claims of the domain, and one for node claims (no functional change).

This is followed by the implementation of multi-node claims, the OCaml
bindings for it, and a comprehensive low-level test suite. The series
also passes our high-level regression test suites.

Patch Summary
-------------

The series contains the following patches:

1. docs/design:      Add design documentation for NUMA-aware claim sets
2. xen/page_alloc:   Extract releasing global claims into a function
3. xen/page_alloc:   Recall global claims if needed when offlining pages
4. xen/page_alloc:   Fix to add unclaimed pages when allocating with claims
5. xen/page_alloc:   Optimize getting per-NUMA-node free page counts
6. xen/page_alloc:   Split d->outstanding_pages: global and per-node claims
7. xen, tools:       Introduce NUMA-aware multi-node claim sets
8. tools/ocaml/libs: Add the OCaml binding for xc_domain_claim_memory()
9. tools/tests:      Add low-level tests for NUMA-aware multi-node claim sets

Review
------

Given the significant changes in this series to support multi-node claim sets,
I recommend reviewing it on its own rather than incrementally.
